Rachel Carson

"One way to open your eyes is to ask yourself, 'What if I had never seen this before? What if I knew I would never see it again?'"

Rachel Carson, a renowned naturalist and writer, is most famous for her book Silent Spring , which increase raise public awareness around the negative consequences - to ecosystems and to human health - of chemical pesticides such as DDT. Before writing Silent Spring, Carson studied marine biology; she studied at Woods Hole Laboratory and pursued a master's degree in Zoology at John Hopkins University before landing a position as a biologist with the Bureau of Fisheries (now the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service), where she wrote a radio series about their marine research. A talented science writer, she wrote several books for the general public about the ocean, including The Sea Around Us , Under the Sea Wind , and The Edge of the Sea , before writing Silent Spring . Carson and her book Silent Spring were a catalyst in forming the modern environmentalist movement.

Interesting Facts

  1. Carson was an alumna of a women's college; she graduated from the Pennsylvania College for Women (now Chatham University) in 1929.
  2. Carson studied English at the Pennsylvania College for Women before shifting her focus to biology.
  3. After running into difficulties with her MA research comparing cranial nerves in reptiles, Carson eventually finished a thesis on urinary organ development in catfish.
  4. Seven years after Carson testified in Congress, the Kennedy administration established the Environmental Protection Agency to, among other tasks, further study the effects of DDT.
